# HEAL Praise Audio — Copyright & Cost Analysis (Path A)

**Date:** 2026-06-30
**Question:** What are the legal + cost implications of using real public-domain hymns in HEAL?

## TL;DR

- **Public-domain hymns (pre-1925 US):** FREE, zero legal risk. ✅ SAFE
- **Post-1925 hymns (most "modern praise"):** Have 3 copyright layers — tune, text, arrangement. Each expires separately.
- **The famous hymns we want (Amazing Grace, Be Still My Soul, etc.):** 100% public domain. ✅ SAFE
- **Some modern hymns (How Great Thou Art English 1953, In Christ Alone 2002):** Partly copyrighted — need careful sourcing.
- **Real cost:** $0 if we stick to pre-1925 hymns. $50-500 if we license 1-2 modern worship collections.

## The 3-layer copyright rule (every hymn has 3 parts)

Every hymn = **tune (melody) + text (lyrics) + arrangement (specific performance)**.

Each layer has its own copyright and expiry:

| Layer | What | Public domain when (US) |
|---|---|---|
| **Tune (melody)** | The notes | Composer died 70+ years ago, OR published 95+ years ago |
| **Text (lyrics)** | The words | Author died 70+ years ago, OR published 95+ years ago |
| **Arrangement (specific recording)** | The performance, the orchestration, the singer's interpretation | 95 years after publication in US (sound recordings before 1924 are public domain) |

**ALL THREE must be public domain (or you have a license) for the recording to be freely distributable.**

## US public-domain day timeline

- 2024: works from 1928 entered PD
- 2025: works from 1929 entered PD (+ sound recordings from 1924)
- **2026 (current):** works from 1930 entered PD
- 2027: works from 1931 enter PD
- 2028: works from 1932 enter PD
- 2029: works from 1933 enter PD (sound recordings from 1928)
- 2030: works from 1934 enter PD (sound recordings from 1929)

**In practical terms:** any hymn tune or text written **before 1930** is in the public domain in the US as of 2026. Any hymn from before 1924 is also public domain in sound recording form (i.e. recordings made before 1924).

## Risk for HEAL

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Use a copyrighted hymn recording without a license | **HIGH** (DMCA takedown + lawsuit possible) | Only use PD sources verified above. Document license + source URL in PB record. |
| Modern arrangement copyright (e.g. Hine's 1953 English of How Great Thou Art) | **MEDIUM** | Use original Swedish text + a different (PD) tune, OR get permission. |
| Performance rights organizations (ASCAP/BMI) | **LOW** | ASCAP/BMI require performance licensing for public venues (churches, concerts). HEAL is app streaming — different license model (CC0 / PD source recordings). |
| International rights (UK, EU, Japan) | **MEDIUM** | PD varies by jurisdiction. US life+70, EU life+70, Japan life+70, Canada life+70. Most pre-1930 hymns are PD everywhere. Document jurisdiction per song. |
| "Compilation" copyright on multi-artist albums | **LOW** | Use single-track downloads from sources that release tracks individually as CC0/PD. |

1. **License attribution** in the PB HEAL_praise record:
   - `source`: archive.org / hymnserve.com / etc.
   - `license`: "Public Domain" / "CC0" / "CC-BY-3.0"
   - `source_url`: direct download URL
   - `recording_year`: when the recording was made
2. **HEAL Terms of Service** should mention that some songs are public domain, some under CCLI/OneLicense
3. **Per-song attribution shown in player UI** (small text: "Recorded by LibriVox volunteers, public domain")
4. **Keep records of every download** in `web/scripts/praise-audio-sources.log`
